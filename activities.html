Bhima Koregaon War: A story of Self Respect, Esteem and Fight Against 'Manusmruti'

Bhima Koregaon war was fought on 1st January 1818 between 2nd Battalion 1st Regiment of the Bombay Native Light Infantry and Peshwa soldiers. In this infantry there were only 500 soldiers and most of them were from Mahar caste and others from Mang & Muslim. At the opposite site there were more than 28000 Peshwa soldiers. It was started on 31st December 1817 at night and ended ston 1 January 1818. Battle ended not only with the victory over Peshwa but it became responsible for the end of Peshwai in Maharashtra.

To remember this day, Dr. B R Ambedkar used to visit Bhima Koregaon every year on 1st January to pay homage and to salute the great Mahar soldiers.

Ambedkar Student Association (ASA) has decided to follow the tradition of Dr. B R Ambedkar every year and pay homage and salute the great Mahar soldiers with the student community on 1st January 2018.

Eligibility: Student who has knowledge about History of Bhima Koregaon will be given preference

About us

The Ambedkar Student’s Association was founded in 1993 by a small group of Dalit students at Hyderabad Central University, led by PhD scholar Rajasekhar. “It was a time when the University had a ‘no politics’ policy, and didn’t allow any posters or students organisations,”

But before the ASA, there was the Progressive Students’ Forum, a group formed in 1990 when attempts were made to implement the Mandal Commission’s recommendations of caste-based reservations in government jobs.
